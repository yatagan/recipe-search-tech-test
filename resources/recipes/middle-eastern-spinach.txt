Middle Eastern spinach
Introduction:
Variations on this side dish are eaten across the Middle East. Itâ€™s good with pitta and chickpeas and/or grilled lamb or chicken. For an optional extra, sprinkle with toasted, chopped walnuts. 
Ingredients:
250-300g spinach, washed well, leaves stripped from any tough stalks oil for frying 1 onion, finely diced 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped 1 tsp black mustard seeds 1 tsp cumin seeds, toasted & ground, or just use ground approx. 300-400g plain yoghurt cayenne 1 lemon salt & pepper
Method:
Put a large pan of water on to boil. Boil the spinach for 1-2 mins, until wilted. Drain, run it under cold water to cool, squeeze out excess moisture and chop it up. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a frying pan. Gently fry the onion for 10 mins, stirring often. Add the garlic, mustard seeds and cumin. Cook for 2 mins. Stir in the spinach. Add enough yoghurt until it becomes a thick, creamy mixture. Season with cayenne, lemon juice, salt and pepper, all to taste.
