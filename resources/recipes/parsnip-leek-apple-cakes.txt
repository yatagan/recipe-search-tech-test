Parsnip, leek and apple cakes
Introduction:
Quick, cheap and yummy. Try these delicate little cakes with noodles, or with a tomato salad side, or with grilled mushrooms, or a poached egg on top. They're great vegetarian fare for breakfast, lunch or dinner. Add in a pinch of chilli flakes if you're partial to a bit of heat. 
Ingredients:
3 parsnips 1 leek, thinly sliced butter & oil for frying 1 apple, grated 1 tsp flour pinch of cinnamon 1 tbsp Parmesan or Pecorino, grated 50g plain flour 1 egg, beaten 100g breadcrumbs salt & pepper
Method:
Top and tail parsnips and boil with skins on until cooked through. Drain, allow to cool, scrape off skin, then mash with a fork. Cook the leeks in a little butter and oil, add grated apple and cook through for a few minutes. Add to mashed parsnips with the teaspoon of flour, cinnamon and seasoning, Parmesan and a knob of butter. Form into flat, round cakes. Coat in flour, then egg and breadcrumbs. Fry in butter and oil in batches until crisp on the outside.
