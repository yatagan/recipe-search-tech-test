Pangrattato (garlic crumbs)
Introduction:
Pangrattato (literally 'grated bread') is called 'poor man's Parmesan', but it doesn't taste like second best. Toasted in garlic and oil, crispy breadcrumbs add a remarkable taste and texture to dishes. 
Ingredients:
3-4 fat slices stale bread, crusts removed olive oil 2-3 garlic cloves, peeled & finely chopped Pinch of dried chilli flakes 1 lemon, zested Salt & pepper
Method:
Blitz the bread in a food processor to coarse crumbs. Warm 2 tablespoons of oil in a frying pan, add the garlic and stir for a few seconds, then add the breadcrumbs. Fry over a medium heat for 4-5 minutes, until golden and crisp. Remove from the heat. Stir in chilli flakes, lemon zest and salt and pepper, all to taste.
